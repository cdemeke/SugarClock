FAQ

Frequently asked questions

Answers to the most common questions about BG TC001.

General

BG TC001 is free, open-source software that turns an inexpensive Ulanzi TC001 pixel clock (~$40) into a dedicated glucose monitor display. It connects to your Dexcom CGM (or Nightscout) over WiFi and shows your current glucose reading in big, colorful numbers on the clock's LED display.

No. BG TC001 is not a medical device and should not be used to make treatment decisions. Always rely on your CGM receiver, CGM app, or blood glucose meter for dosing insulin or making medical decisions. BG TC001 is a convenience display only.

BG TC001 works with Dexcom CGMs (G6, G7, ONE, etc.) through the Dexcom Share feature. It also works with any CGM that sends data to a Nightscout server or similar JSON API. This means it can work with Libre sensors through xDrip+ and Nightscout, or any other setup that provides glucose data through a web endpoint.

The total cost is just the price of the Ulanzi TC001 clock, which is typically $35–50 on Amazon. The BG TC001 software is completely free and open source. There are no subscriptions, no monthly fees, and no hidden costs.

No coding experience is needed! The setup involves typing a few commands into your computer's terminal (we show you exactly what to type) and editing one line in a text file to set your WiFi password. After the initial setup, everything is configured through a friendly web page you open in your browser.

Setup & Installation

The setup works on Windows, Mac, and Linux computers. You only need the computer for the one-time initial setup. After that, the clock runs independently and you configure it through your phone or any web browser.

Don't worry! You can always start over. If something goes wrong during the flash, just try the upload command again. The worst that can happen is the clock shows a blank screen — in which case you just re-flash it. And if you want to go back to the original clock software, you can restore the factory firmware (see below).

Yes! If you backed up the factory firmware before flashing BG TC001 (we recommend this in the setup guide), you can restore it with one command:

esptool.py -p /dev/cu.usbserial-1410 -b 460800 write_flash 0x0 tc001_factory_backup.bin

Replace the port (/dev/cu.usbserial-1410) with your actual port. On Windows this will be something like COM3.

Yes, flashing BG TC001 replaces the original Ulanzi firmware. That's why we recommend backing it up first (see the Setup Guide). The backup file lets you restore the original software at any time.

Daily use

By default, the clock fetches a new glucose reading every 60 seconds. You can change this in the configuration (the minimum is 15 seconds). Most CGMs provide a new reading every 5 minutes, so updating more than once per minute doesn't add much benefit.

No! After the initial setup, the clock only needs power (any USB-C charger or port will work) and a WiFi connection. You can unplug it from your computer and place it anywhere in your home that has WiFi and a power outlet.

The Ulanzi TC001 has a small internal battery, but it's not designed for extended battery operation — especially with WiFi active and the LEDs lit. For reliable operation, keep it plugged into USB power at all times. Think of it as a plug-in desk clock, not a portable device.

Absolutely! Open any web browser on a device connected to the same WiFi and go to http://[clock-ip-address]/config.html. You can change thresholds, colors, alerts, brightness, and everything else — no computer or terminal needed. Your settings are saved on the clock and survive power cycles and firmware updates.

Yes! The clock is a physical display — anyone in the room can see it. You can also access the web dashboard from any device on your WiFi network to see your current reading, trend graph, and history. Great for parents keeping an eye on their child's glucose.

Your glucose data flows directly from your data source (Dexcom or Nightscout) to the clock over HTTPS (encrypted). The clock does not send your data to any third-party servers. Your Dexcom credentials and WiFi password are stored locally on the clock's internal memory and never leave your network. The web dashboard is only accessible on your local WiFi.

Technical

No, the ESP32 chip in the Ulanzi TC001 only supports 2.4 GHz WiFi. Most home routers broadcast both 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz networks. Look for your network name that does not have "5G" in it, or check your router settings. If you're unsure, your WiFi network name without any suffix is usually the 2.4 GHz one.

Connect the clock to your computer again and run:

cd BG_TC001
git pull
pio run --target upload
pio run --target uploadfs

Your settings (WiFi, Dexcom login, thresholds, etc.) are preserved across updates — you won't have to reconfigure anything.

Each clock connects to one data source at a time (either Dexcom Share or a custom URL). If you want to monitor multiple people, you'd need one clock per person. They're affordable enough to have several!

There are a few ways:

  • Connect the clock to your computer via USB and run pio device monitor — look for the [WIFI] Connected! line.
  • Check your WiFi router's admin page for connected devices — look for a device named "ESP32" or similar.
  • Use a network scanner app on your phone (like "Fing" on iOS/Android) to find devices on your network.
